<objective>
Refactor the `outline_text` engine in `treesitter_ast.py` from a flat root-children loop into a
recursive `visit()` that transparently descends `unwrap` wrapper kinds and emits first-lines for
`keep_first_line` data-key kinds — while preserving exact backward compatibility for the 12 existing
languages. Then tune the existing `bash` config and add a new `toml` config (both work against
top-level children + the new first-line emit mechanism).

Purpose: This is the structural foundation for all of Phase 17. SQL/YAML/JSON (Plan 17-02) cannot
produce any outline without the `unwrap` descent because their declarations are buried in wrapper
nodes. Bash and TOML are the two languages whose declarations sit at the top level and so validate the
engine without needing deep descent — they de-risk the refactor before 17-02 layers on the harder
grammars.

Output: Generalized `outline_text` engine, extended `LangCfg` dataclass, tuned `bash` + new `toml`
`_LANG_CONFIG` entries, updated shell test, and a new TOML test. All 8 pre-existing outline tests stay
green.
</objective>
